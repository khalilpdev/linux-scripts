Depending on your specific setup or driver version, one of these commands might be more effective for your system.

For Vulkan Games (and most modern titles): This is the standard, fully explicit command.
__NV_PRIME_RENDER_OFFLOAD=1 __GLX_VENDOR_LIBRARY_NAME=nvidia __VK_LAYER_NV_optimus=NVIDIA_only %command%

For OpenGL Games (or older titles): The __GLX variable is the only one required for older OpenGL applications.
__NV_PRIME_RENDER_OFFLOAD=1 __GLX_VENDOR_LIBRARY_NAME=nvidia %command%

For Problematic or Specific Games: If the standard command fails, you can try filtering by the exact GPU name reported by your system (find this by running nvidia-smi in the terminal).
DXVK_FILTER_DEVICE_NAME="NVIDIA GeForce ..." %command%

🔧 Como forçar o uso da NVIDIA
Solução 1: Configuração via Xorg (recomendada)
Crie/edite o arquivo de configuração do Xorg:

bash
sudo nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf.d/20-nvidia.conf
Cole este conteúdo:

text
Section "Device"
    Identifier "NVIDIA Card"
    Driver "nvidia"
    VendorName "NVIDIA Corporation"
    BusID "PCI:1:0:0"
    Option "PrimaryGPU" "yes"
EndSection
Importante: O BusID deve corresponder ao do seu nvidia-smi. Nele mostra 00000000:01:00.0, então o BusID é PCI:1:0:0.

Salve e reinicie sua sessão (logout/login ou reboot)..
